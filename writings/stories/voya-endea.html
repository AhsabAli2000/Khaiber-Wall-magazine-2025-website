A voyage to my endeavours

'Today, you are well acquainted with a prominent figure. Nadir Hamza. You wait days after days to be enlisted in his fanbase. He is celebrated as an icon amongst the youngsters. Now I cordially request, our honourable guest Nadir Hamza to come over the stage. Please. give a big hand for Nadir Hamza, the anchor was at loud. I could not burst into happy tears or electrifying shouts. With pride, I adorned the podium. About three hundred fifty eyes stared at me altogether. I started uttering and could remember. my legs were no more shaky. But those very legs once and again betrayed me when I was only a boy of thirteen. I was not a gold spoon fed boy but I was dedicated to prove my worth. As I was the eldest son of my parents, they consciously or subconsciously thought that I would make our family solvent with a magic wand I was a boy of timid nature In college. I had countless chances to be a recognized personality among the fellow mates. But I was not enough confident, courageous and above all outspoken. My mentors, the promising teachers had been a shade above my head. Till now even death, I will recall two teachers in my lifetime. But they are entitled in my diary in different ways. But now, each moments send me to those Flashback. Mr. Intekhab, a smart, prudent, dashing man, did not take pressure to provide knowledge. He never compromised with anything out of the prescribed rules. He was not a classroom traditional teacher, he was a mentor, guide, motivator. He never allowed me to be extracted from the list of the speakers in the classroom public speaking session. Sometimes, I, the most polite boy, tried to give him a bluff. Day by day, even I could not tolerate his remarkable attention on me. Memories are inscribed but that memory is inscribed in too deep. It was 8:30 am. The loudest bell I heard. "Oh! I should skip my turn anyway. I cannot be insulted more! I have self esteem at least." Mr. Intekhab Morshed greeted with bold vocal, 'Good morning, boys.' He announced. "Today I will go for a lottery and the first person will adorn the lecture for 10 minutes. A little hope peeped. I started praying to the Almighty. "Please, save me. Mr. Intekhab smiled and I could see his twinkling eyes. I approached to the lecture but I felt only trembling. My legs were so shaky that I could not breath well. Topic was, The person you will remember most.' I could not utter a single word for first three minutes. After three minutes, Mr. Intekhab's vocal Tsunami ruined my last hope. The limitless insult I could not bear. With the blessings of my weakest English, I started my speech. I talked about Mr. Intekhab whom I would remember for working behind my progress. Suddenly. Mr. Intekhab interrupted me and he requested all to applause. I got frightened that surely he was agitated. But he was not at all. With contempt, Mr. Intekhab told me. Be bored by me always. That man led me to the path of new journey and I travelled. Another one. I can not keep him away from my gratitude list. He is none other than my father. The person always believed that I had the capability to achieve if I had efforts. He could not be aware of my study. And finally I tried and chosen a great way to be felt, thought. discussed and honoured. Nelson Mandela was my icon growing up. One of his quotes goes like this, "It seems impossible until it is done." Now when I look back. I have made possible the impossible one being an influencial public speaker. Another one of his saying which I can relate to is, "A good head and a good heart are always a Formidable combination." I realize now, joint venture of those two persons, a good headed teacher and a good hearted father successfully made me - a formidable combination. I will never cease to remember them.
